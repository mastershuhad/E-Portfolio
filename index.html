Reflect

Reflections

The following reflections document key learning experiences from my undergraduate studies, structured to capture the experience, key learning outcomes, challenges encountered, solutions applied, and areas identified for future improvement.

Group Work & Collaboration

Reflection 1: Group Project Experience

Experience: During my undergraduate studies, I participated in a collaborative web development project in which our team was required to design and develop a functional application. This experience provided valuable insight into how group-based academic projects operate within a computing context and highlighted the importance of structured teamwork.

Learning: Through this experience, I developed a deeper understanding of the significance of clear communication and shared responsibility within a team setting. I observed how each member's individual strengths could be effectively leveraged to produce a more complete and well-rounded academic outcome. Additionally, I gained practical experience in applying technical knowledge acquired through lectures to a real development task.

Challenges: A significant challenge encountered was coordinating tasks effectively, as team members operated under varying schedules and adopted different approaches to the assigned work. This occasionally resulted in delays in completing certain components and required additional coordination to maintain overall project progress.

Solutions: To address these coordination difficulties, the team established a shared timeline with clearly defined roles and submission deadlines for each member. Regular check-in meetings were also arranged to monitor progress, facilitate discussion, and ensure that all members remained accountable for their respective contributions.

Future Improvements: In future group projects, I intend to take a more proactive role in facilitating team discussions and to advocate for establishing clear communication structures and task-management systems from the outset. This will contribute to a more efficient and collaborative working process.

Practical & Laboratory Work

Reflection 2: Laboratory Sessions and Practical Work

Experience: Laboratory sessions have formed an integral component of my academic programme, providing structured opportunities to engage in hands-on activities related to programming, circuit design, and system analysis. These sessions complemented the theoretical content delivered through lectures and enabled practical engagement with core technical concepts.

Learning: Through these activities, I developed a more comprehensive understanding of how to apply theoretical knowledge to practical situations, troubleshoot technical errors systematically, and follow established procedures with precision. I also gained an appreciation for the importance of methodical working practices and technical accuracy in an engineering and computing context.

Challenges: A recurring challenge experienced during laboratory sessions was encountering unexpected errors or obtaining incorrect outputs, which initially required considerable time to diagnose and resolve. This was at times frustrating, particularly when it necessitated repeating sections of the experiment.

Solutions: To address these difficulties, I adopted the practice of thoroughly reviewing laboratory manuals and related materials prior to each session. I also made greater use of available support by consulting with lecturers and fellow students when faced with technical difficulties, which improved my ability to identify and resolve issues efficiently.

Future Improvements: I aim to enhance my preparation before each laboratory session and to further develop my problem-solving capabilities so that I can address technical challenges with greater confidence and efficiency. I also intend to improve my ability to document and analyse experimental results more rigorously.

Academic Communication

Reflection 3: Communication and Presentation Skills (DELT Module)

Experience: The DELT module provided structured opportunities throughout the academic year to develop communication and presentation competencies through formal presentations, impromptu speaking tasks, and group discussions. These activities formed a significant component of the module and required consistent engagement and preparation.

Learning: Through regular participation in these activities, I developed the ability to organise ideas in a logical and coherent manner, apply appropriate academic language, and present information clearly to an audience. I also improved my capacity to respond spontaneously during impromptu speaking tasks, which required the ability to think critically and communicate effectively under time constraints.

Challenges: I initially experienced a degree of nervousness during formal presentations and, in particular, during impromptu speaking activities that required immediate and unscripted responses. This occasionally affected my fluency and the overall clarity of my delivery.

Solutions: To address this, I engaged in regular speaking practice, prepared structured key points prior to formal presentations, and actively participated in all available classroom speaking opportunities to gradually build confidence and reduce anxiety. The constructive feedback provided by the module lecturer was also highly beneficial in identifying specific areas for improvement.

Future Improvements: I intend to continue developing my public speaking skills, with a particular focus on delivering more fluent and well-structured presentations. I also aim to improve my ability to respond with greater clarity during spontaneous speaking tasks and to make more effective use of visual aids to communicate complex ideas.
